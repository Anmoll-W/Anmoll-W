### Learning in Public (**[Anmoll-W/blog](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog)**)

I distill working sessions into posts on systems, silent bugs, and shipping with AI tools. Every post comes from something that actually happened.


| Title | Covers 
|---|---|
| [Upgrading My AI Agent Roster, and the Baseline I Forgot to Save](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog/blob/main/posts/personas-180-of-180-and-no-baseline.md) | A depth upgrade on top of the six-persona vault roster: a dissent protocol that requires every agent to steelman an alternative, a fourteen-pattern Anmoll-watchlist, a twelve-phrase banned-list that triggers a gate failure, per-persona refusal catalogs (seven to eight triggers each), per-persona failure-mode lists (ten to fourteen named modes each), and a six-axis structural eval rubric with a department-head bar at twenty-four out of thirty. The eval scored every persona thirty out of thirty — one hundred eighty out of one hundred eighty — and that ceiling is exactly what exposed the problem. The rubric checks file-on-disk properties, not live behavior, and because the vault was never git-snapshotted before the upgrade, there is no pre-upgrade version of any persona to compare against. Ships a forty-seven-line `persona-snapshot.sh` that runs weekly with eight-week retention so the next upgrade has a real before-and-after.
| [Four Agents Went RED for Three Weeks. I Cut the Team in Half.](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog/blob/main/posts/four-agents-went-red.md) | Three consecutive RED weekly evals on the same four agents exposed memory-based rules as non-load-bearing. The fix ran as three coupled tracks: thirteen personas consolidated to six (with absorbed domains ported as labeled sections in host memory files), all thirty-six skills moved from implicit ownership to an explicit primary plus also-invocable-by routing matrix, and a four-step Brain Protocol installed — token-bounded pre-action retrieval (≤35 lines, grep-only, session-cached), ReAct loop, mandatory fenced gate block per persona, and a Pending Validation memory write that Vera promotes weekly. A parallel six-agent audit caught seven stale references the consolidation sweep missed and surfaced the pattern that index files and rubric prose drift independently of routing tables. Post-consolidation eval: 6/6 GREEN.
| [The Claims Were There. The Sources Were Not.](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog/blob/main/posts/claims-without-sources.md) | Reading PM Brain OS, finding the 20% the vault was missing, and what a Vera decision eval caught before it shipped. Covers conservative-floor provenance tagging (default to intuition, require positive evidence to climb), vault-wide hypothesis lifecycle tracking across four projects with 7d warning and 30d auto-kill, and the /prep ritual that surfaces hypothesis-decision conflicts before stakeholder meetings.
| [I Was Burning 18 Claude Sessions a Day. Here Is What Found Them.](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog/blob/main/posts/token-burn-audit.md) | Five silent automation bugs in a LaunchAgent-based vault OS, each invisible because it was the absence of a signal rather than the presence of an error. A done-file contract that only accepted exit 0 turned one weekly job into 18 daily sessions. A Perl alarm timer that does not survive exec gave a 2am consolidator a seven-hour nightly runtime. Two zombie agents whose self-disable logic ran after exec. And a BOOT context load that had grown to 180,000 characters with autoCompact never enabled.
| [We Designed a Multi-Model Router. Then We Asked One Question.](https://github.com/Anmoll-W/blog/blob/main/posts/right-model-wrong-problem.md) | A full session of multi-model architecture research, a Vera decision eval, a Gemini adversarial BLOCK overturned by a 15-minute CLI test — and the one question that reduced the whole system to two runner modifications and one new file. Gemini Flash for live web-grounded research; everything else stays on Claude Max. Covers the test-the-blocker-first pattern, the "do you even need this?" question, and why the right scope is the one that fills a real capability gap, not a theoretical one
